Common questions

MuseumLog uses your iCloud account to sync. To check the basics:

  • Both devices are signed into the same Apple ID.
  • Settings → [your name] → iCloud → iCloud Drive is on.
  • MuseumLog is enabled in iCloud → Apps using iCloud.
  • Both devices have a working internet connection (CloudKit sync requires it).

Sync is usually within a minute, but can take longer for large photo sets. If everything looks right and visits still aren't appearing after ten minutes, force-quit MuseumLog and reopen it.

The good news: if iCloud Photos is on, your photos are still in your library — they just need to re-attach to each visit.

MuseumLog will try to restore photos automatically the first time you open a visit on the new device. For that to work:

  • iCloud Photos must be on (Settings → [your name] → iCloud → Photos).
  • MuseumLog must have Photos library access (Settings → MuseumLog → Photos → Full Access).
  • Your photos must have finished downloading from iCloud (it can take a while on a fresh device).

Visits logged before this feature shipped (May 2026) won't restore automatically — they pre-date the cross-device identifier. We're working on a one-time migration tool; in the meantime, you can re-attach photos manually from the visit detail page.

Our atlas covers the world's 50 most-visited museums — anywhere else, just type the name and tap "Add" or hit return. Free-text museums work everywhere in the app: they show up in your logbook, your passport, and your statistics. They just don't have pre-populated location data.

If you visit a niche museum a lot and would like it added to the atlas, email us — we expand the list when there's clear demand.

The Profile tab (rightmost in the tab bar) has both options: Logbook Colour and Logbook Font. Themes and fonts are part of MuseumLog Pro.

Subscriptions are managed by Apple, not MuseumLog. To cancel:

  • Open Settings → tap your name at the top → Subscriptions.
  • Find MuseumLog and tap Cancel Subscription.

Your Pro features remain until the end of the current billing period. Lifetime purchases are not subscriptions and don't need cancelling.

Refunds for App Store purchases are handled by Apple — we have no ability to issue them directly. Apple's process is at reportaproblem.apple.com. Most refund requests within 14 days of purchase are approved.

Two options, depending on how thorough you want to be:

  • Delete a single visit — open the visit, tap the menu, choose Delete.
  • Delete everything locally — uninstall the app from your iPhone.
  • Delete the iCloud copy too — Settings → [your name] → iCloud → Manage Account Storage → MuseumLog → Delete Data.

We don't store your data on our servers, so there's nothing extra to request. See the Privacy Policy for the full picture.
